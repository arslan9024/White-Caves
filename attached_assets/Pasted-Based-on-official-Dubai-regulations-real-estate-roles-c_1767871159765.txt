Based on official Dubai regulations, real estate roles can be categorized into two main types: specific career paths for professionals and the formal list of activities that a company must be licensed to perform by the **Dubai Land Department (DLD) and the Real Estate Regulatory Agency (RERA)**.

Here is an overview of the key professional roles you might find within a Dubai real estate company:

| Professional Role | Core Responsibilities | Key Requirements / Notes |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Real Estate Agent/Broker** | Facilitates property sales, purchases, and leases; acts as an intermediary between buyers and sellers. | Must be sponsored by a RERA-licensed brokerage, complete DREI training, pass the RERA exam, and obtain a license. They earn commission-based income (typically 2-5%). |
| **Real Estate Developer** | Acquires land, plans, finances, and oversees construction projects for sale or lease. | Must register with RERA and open an escrow account for off-plan sales to protect buyer funds. Can be a **Principal Developer** (develops the main project) or **Sub-Developer** (develops part of a larger project). |
| **Property Manager** | Manages day-to-day operations of buildings or communities, including maintenance, tenant relations, fee collection, and financial reporting. | For managing third-party properties, the company must provide a substantial bank guarantee (e.g., AED 5 million). Often works under a "Jointly Owned Property Management" license. |
| **Real Estate Valuer** | Determines the market value of properties for sales, financing, investment, or tax purposes. | Requires a specific "Real Estate Valuation Services" license from RERA. |
| **Real Estate Consultant** | Provides expert advice on market trends, investment opportunities, legal matters, and property strategy. | Often requires a "Real Estate Consultancy" license. |

### üìä Licensing Comparison: Broker vs. Property Management Company
| Aspect | Real Estate Broker (Individual) | Property Management Company |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Key Activity** | Brokering sales, purchases, and leases of real estate. | Leasing and managing properties owned by third parties. |
| **Governing Body** | Dubai Land Department (DLD) / Real Estate Regulatory Agency (RERA). | DLD / RERA, and Department of Economy and Tourism (DET). |
| **Primary Requirement** | Must be **sponsored by a licensed brokerage**; freelance work is not permitted. | For a mainland license managing third-party properties, a **bank guarantee of AED 5 million** is required. |
| **Training** | Complete mandatory 4-day training by the **Dubai Real Estate Institute (DREI)**. | Company managers must complete **RERA-approved training** and pass an exam. |
| **Key Steps** | 1. Obtain residency visa via employer. <br>2. Complete DREI training & pass RERA exam. <br>3. Employer submits application via DLD's **Trakheesi** system. | 1. Choose business jurisdiction (Mainland/Free Zone). <br>2. Get initial approval & trade name from DED. <br>3. Train manager & get RERA card. <br>4. Finalize license, rent office, register with RERA. |
| **Typical Cost** | ~AED 2,500-3,000 (exam, card, fees). Renewal: ~AED 510/year. | Setup: ~**AED 80,000-150,000** for mainland. <br>Annual License Fees: **AED 15,000** for third-party management. |
| **Timeline** | A few weeks (after securing employment and training). | Approximately **8 weeks** from start to finish. |

### üìù Key Considerations Before Applying
*   **Visa and Sponsorship**: You must have a valid UAE residency visa to work. Brokers get this through their sponsoring brokerage. Property management company owners can obtain a visa through their business.
*   **No Freelance Option**: You cannot operate as a freelance real estate agent in Dubai; working under a licensed company is mandatory.
*   **Central System**: All applications are processed through government online portals (DLD's **Trakheesi** for professional licenses and DET for business setup).
*   **Jurisdiction Choice**: For property management, your choice between a **Mainland** license (allows operating across Dubai but requires a local office and higher capital) and a **Free Zone** license (100% foreign ownership, but operations may be geographically restricted) is critical.

You can **start the process** for either path by preparing the required documents (passport copies, educational certificates, etc.) and ensuring you meet the basic eligibility criteria, such as being at least 21 years old.
